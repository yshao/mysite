How I come to pointcloud registration

I’ve been working for many years, on a disciplined procedure for integrative merging between biomedical data and deep learning. To address this, I’ve been working with my advisor @ WPI to solve the fundamental problems that exist in multimodal dataset.

Since data preprocessing and analysis could take up 85% of the time, we find this an area of opportunity for practical optimization. For heterogeneous, multi-modal data, this challenging and time-consuming task shares the fundamental problem in registration. A common and similar use-case is the navigation of autonomous vehicles, from which multi-modal datasets are plentiful and far less sparse and hence spurred much progress in computer vision methods for multi-modal data.

Pointcloud is an N-dimensional dataset to perform research in multi-variate and multi-dimensional optimization. The bulk of my research is optimization methods on the registration problem - concretely, to improve upon the non-convexity of corresponding low dimensions, ie. the observational views of data. Since registration is a fundamental problem, it will show improvement on any downstream problem with any dataset, ie. domain adaptation, score ranking, tracking, etc.

Therefore, I’m highly motivated to practical medical applications that could bring to data science the rigor of human reasoning and knowledge from the brain science/health science space.

Zero-shot visual recognition

An area of immense interest is to connect the mechanisms that lead to safe and reliable machine learning/deep learning system. To this end, visual and semantics reasoning in the processing of the multi-modal representation is necessary for the compositionality of “objectness”. To exhibit robustness in this type of recognition system is the primary aim of zero-shot recognition.
