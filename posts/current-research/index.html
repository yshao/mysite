Digital Health part 1

I work at Mass General Hospital Neurology Department in a clinical data analytics group (CDAC - clinical data animation center) with PI neurologist Dr. Brandon Westover where neurophysiology/computational neuroscience/clinical science intersect. Working at CDAC gave me the opportunity to interact with physicians, postdocs, and biomedical analysts with specialized knowledge of the dynamical system of the human brain physiology. To find machine learning/deep learning models that work well, it involves curation of data to get concept representations learned from clinical data, forming a database of metadata. This compositional data concept system can be harnessed to build machine learning algorithm for many detection and prediction of degenerative states of the brain physiology and medical informatics.

My typical projects involve wrangling multimodal data source ranging from waveforms like EEG/EKG, clinical timeseries like labs/vitals, sequentially updated physician notes, and prepare them into research ready data through data cleaning, interrogating data statistics, rule-based label harvesting, data pattern classification with models, and discovering data correlation through predictive modeling and data exploration.

Projects I have been involved include but not limited to: Prediction of onset of sepsis with streaming electronics health records. Large scale visualization of spatio-temporal patterns from seizure burden data. Delirium predictive analytics on electronics health records. Big analytics on EEG processing. * Automated Surveillance for Ventilator Associated Events.

Sepsis-onset Detection 25 november 2018 time-sensitive detection for near-time application

Predictive algorithm becomes policy relevant what it’s also predicting the onset, not just the category. This makes it relevant for interventional study.

This collaboration investigates the deployment of sepsis onset detection within ICU. Sepsis is a common precursor to neurological disorders. The early detection of its onset could provide opportunity for intervention within ICU healthcare.

To train the deep learning algorithm for the detection, I prepared a cohort of 7800 patients found within ICU with sepsis condition as determined by the Sepsis-III definition (published by CDC). Then working with the clinical informatics group at Emory to develop a model that predicts the onset of sepsis.

The project introduces the importance of temporal context. For example, antibiotics medications are frequently given to patients within ICU, however, if antibiotics are given with certain number of times over 3 days when followed by microbiology culture test, then the medication was for sepsis treatment.
