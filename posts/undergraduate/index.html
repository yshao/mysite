Prelude

Nanotech, Optics and Robotics

In my undergraduate, I was interested in everything nano, optical, abstract invariant principles of the physical world. The “goal project” was creating self-assemblying nano-fabricators. The possibilities at creating a abundance society appealed to me.

I had the fortune to work with a nobel prize winner in nanometerials, Shuji Nakamura, the inventor of blue LED, that summer was both fun and informative, as I found out how “wet” work is conducted in a fab room. I learned about van de pauw method of Hall effect measurement and a bit of crystal lattice structure.

Paper

For my undergraduate design project. I invented a innovative apparatus and electrochemistry process to create material for the characterization of laser beam profile with high purity silver. (95% and above, grade above sterling silver, which is at ~93%)

Paper

The silver helical target became part of the patent description in a laser characterization apparatus

Link

I was also a mentor in FIRST Robotics for Team 159 at Poudre High School. I was also a former participant in the robotics competition

It’s one of the formative influences that led to study engineering. We won the Chairman’s Award for profileration of STEM for the state of Colorado and was one of the early teams that made the presence significant for the FIRST committee to form the Colorado Regional.

I’ve learned most about robotics here. The open-source hardware movement later made it cool to use arduino and rasp-pi.

FIRST Website

On team 159, I did robot and motor controls, electronics and was the original webmaster. I learned also machine shop related skills with metal and plastic. The new website is much more organized today!
